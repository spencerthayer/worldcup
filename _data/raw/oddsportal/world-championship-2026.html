FIFA World Cup Games

FIFA World Cup games begin long before the tournament is reached, with the qualification phase spanning multiple confederations (AFC, CAF, CONCACAF, CONMEBOL, OFC, and UEFA). 

Teams compete in regional qualifying tournaments for a long period after the previous tournament, and odds comparisons can be found for all of these FIFA World Cup qualifying games on this page.

As co-hosts for the 2026 World Cup, the United States, Mexico, and Canada automatically qualify, while the remaining 45 spots are made up by confederation qualifiers and inter-confederation playoffs.

The 2026 FIFA World Cup features 48 teams divided into twelve groups which started from the 2026 tournament, with each team playing three World Cup games in the group stage. The tournament's new format sees the top two teams from each group, along with the eight best third-placed teams, advance to the round of 32 for more FIFA World Cup fixtures in the knockout stage.

After the group stage, World Cup fixtures follow a single-elimination format through to the final. Each knockout match must produce a winner, with extra time and penalties also a possibility in each match at this stage. The expanded tournament structure features a huge 104 World Cup games in total, from the opening match through to the FIFA World Cup final.
